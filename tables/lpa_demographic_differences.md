## Interpretation Paragraph (manuscript-ready)

Table 3 presents demographic and organizational differences across the three latent expectancy profiles. The results warrant a cautious interpretation. Regarding demographic variables, gender distribution did not differ significantly across profiles (χ²(2) = 3.43, *p* = .180), although Profile 3 (High Efficiency-Decision) showed a somewhat higher proportion of male respondents (59.6%) compared to Profiles 1 and 2 (52.0% and 49.1%, respectively). Administrative rank exhibited a statistically significant chi-square association with profile membership (χ²(12) = 22.18, *p* = .036, *V* = .172), though the effect size was small; no directional pattern was consistently interpretable. Career tenure did not differ significantly across profiles (*p* = .348). In contrast, motivational and work-related variables showed substantial and statistically significant differences. Intrinsic motivation (F(2, 374) = 46.83, *p* < .001, η² = .200) and perceived work effectiveness (F(2, 374) = 49.59, *p* < .001, η² = .210) increased monotonically from Profile 1 through Profile 3, indicating that profile differentiation is primarily driven by motivational and experiential factors rather than by demographic characteristics. Organizational AI support, however, did not differ significantly across profiles (F(2, 374) = 0.52, *p* = .594, η² = .003), despite its significant role as a predictor of automation expectations within profiles in the supplementary OLS analyses. This pattern suggests that organizational support influences expectancy intensity within a given profile but does not determine which profile an individual occupies.
