## Interpretation Paragraph (manuscript-ready)

Table 2 presents the descriptive characteristics of the three latent expectancy profiles. Several patterns merit attention. First, automation expectancy (Q20_3) is the lowest-rated dimension across all three profiles (range: 2.44–2.84), with less than one scale unit separating the profiles on this dimension. This contrasts sharply with the differentiation observed for efficiency (Q20_1 range: 2.73–5.00) and decision-support expectations (Q20_2 range: 2.93–4.35), confirming that automation skepticism represents a universal characteristic of the sample rather than a profile-defining feature. Second, intrinsic motivation and perceived work effectiveness increase monotonically across profiles in the expected direction (motivation: 3.21 → 3.79 → 4.23; effect: 3.18 → 3.59 → 4.09), consistent with the SDT-EVT mediation pathway identified in the primary OLS analysis. Third, organizational AI support (support_main) shows comparatively minimal variation across profiles (2.79–2.90, F = 0.52, p = .594), suggesting that organizational support context does not determine which expectancy profile an individual occupies, though it may modulate expectancy intensity within profiles (as evidenced by within-profile OLS results).
